Rose M. Schneider

Why is learning hard?

I'm a cognitive scientist passionate about the acquisition of knowledge.

Leveraging quantitative research methods to tackle tough questions about early cognition, I have to think like and design for children. I'm an advocate for technology in cognitive science research, and design behavioral studies on tablets, computers, and Amazon Mechanical Turk.

I'm interested in how we achieve rich and complex knowledge from limited information, whether we're children learning the meanings of numbers, or adults telling a story with data.

I am currently a graduate student at the University of Pennsylvania where I am pursuing on the origins of numerical cognition with Elizabeth Brannon. I was previously a lab manager at the Stanford Language and Cognition Lab under Michael Frank. I completed my undergraduate degree at Johns Hopkins University where I researched cognitive development and visual cognition.
