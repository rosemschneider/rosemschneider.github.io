Rose M. Schneider

Why is learning hard?

I am a graduate student at the University of California, San Diego researching how children acquire number with David Barner. I'm particularly interested in how the fundamentals of symbolic number, such as counting or one-to-one correspondence, are related to complex concepts such as infinity or exact number. Drawing on both within- and cross-cultural data from children, I explore how humans acquire sophisticated conceptual knowledge from sparse data in a noisy world. In other words, I study how we learn what we know, and how learning is (and isn't) hard.

I recently completed a master's degree at the University of Pennsylvania with Liz Brannon, and was previously a lab manager at the Stanford Language and Cognition Lab under Michael Frank. I completed my undergraduate degree at Johns Hopkins University where I researched cognitive development and visual cognition.
