Why is learning hard?

The sensation of learning is familiar. Oftentimes, it can feel difficult, and despite our best efforts, we might not be very good at it. When it comes to learning things like language or number, however, it turns out humans are remarkable and adept learners; somehow, we progress from speechless infants to children and adults using language to express complex concepts, such as mathematics.

Broadly, I'm interested in how we achieve such rich and sophisticated conceptual knowledge from sparse data in a noisy world. In other words, I study how we learn what we know, and how learning is (and isn't) hard.

I am currently a graduate student at the University of California, San Diego researching how and what children learn about number from the structure and content of language with Dave Barner. I recently completed a master's degree at the University of Pennsylvania with Liz Brannon, and was previously a lab manager at the Stanford Language and Cognition Lab under Michael Frank. I completed my undergraduate degree at Johns Hopkins University where I researched cognitive development and visual cognition.
